Brand Overview

MintCheck is a mobile-first vehicle health diagnostic platform that empowers car buyers with transparent, professional-grade vehicle inspections. Our brand embodies trust, clarity, and confidence—helping users make informed decisions when purchasing used vehicles.

Brand Personality

🎯
Clear & Direct
We communicate complex automotive data in simple, actionable terms.
🤝
Trustworthy
We provide honest, unbiased information to build long-term user confidence.
✨
Premium & Modern
Our design reflects quality and sophistication without being pretentious.
💚
Approachable
We make vehicle diagnostics accessible to everyone, regardless of technical knowledge.

Target Audience

MintCheck serves first-time and experienced car buyers who want transparency and confidence when purchasing used vehicles. Our users value data-driven insights and appreciate technology that simplifies complex processes.

Logo Usage

The MintCheck logo is available in two versions to ensure visibility across different backgrounds. Always use the appropriate version based on the background color.

MintCheck
White Logo — Use on dark backgrounds (#1A1A1A, #3EB489, etc.)
MintCheck
Mint Logo — Use on light backgrounds (white, #F8F8F7, etc.)

Logo Guidelines

  • Maintain adequate clear space around the logo (minimum height of the logo on all sides)
  • Do not stretch, distort, or alter the logo proportions
  • Do not apply additional effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
  • Do not change the logo color to anything other than white or mint green
  • Minimum size: 100px width for digital, 0.75" for print

Typography Guidelines

  • Use font-weight 600 for all headings and emphasis
  • Use font-weight 400 for body text and descriptions
  • Never use custom line-height unless absolutely necessary
  • Maintain consistent vertical rhythm with proper spacing
  • Use color to establish hierarchy, not just size

Tone & Voice

MintCheck's voice is confident, clear, and trustworthy. We speak to users as informed advisors, not salespeople or mechanics.

Core Principles

Be Clear, Not Technical
Explain automotive concepts in everyday language. Avoid jargon unless necessary, and always provide context.
Be Honest, Not Alarmist
Present facts objectively. Don't exaggerate problems, but don't downplay them either.
Be Helpful, Not Pushy
Offer guidance and recommendations, but respect that users make their own decisions.
Be Professional, Not Stiff
Maintain professionalism while being approachable and conversational.

Voice Characteristics

We Are We Are Not
Clear and direct Vague or ambiguous
Confident and knowledgeable Arrogant or condescending
Trustworthy and transparent Secretive or misleading
Empowering and supportive Patronizing or dismissive
Professional yet friendly Overly casual or formal

Writing Style

Consistent writing style ensures clarity and builds trust across all MintCheck communications.

Guidelines

✓ Do This
  • Use active voice: "We found 3 trouble codes"
  • Be specific: "Engine temperature is 15°F above normal"
  • Explain implications: "This may cause the vehicle to fail emissions testing"
  • Use contractions for friendliness: "We'll scan the engine"
  • Start with the outcome: "Safe to Buy — No major issues found"
  • Use second person: "Your vehicle's systems are functioning normally"
✗ Don't Do This
  • Use passive voice: "3 trouble codes were found"
  • Be vague: "Temperature seems a bit high"
  • Use jargon without context: "P0300 detected"
  • Be overly formal: "We shall commence scanning"
  • Bury the lead: "After analyzing data, we determined it's safe"
  • Use third person: "The user should check the engine"

Messaging Examples

Headlines & Recommendations

Safe to Buy
Based on the scan, this vehicle's engine and core systems appear to be in good condition. No major concerns were found.
Proceed with Caution
The scan found some items that need attention. Review the details below and consider having a mechanic inspect the vehicle before buying.
Not Recommended
The scan found significant concerns with this vehicle's systems. We recommend looking at other options or getting a professional inspection before proceeding.

Error Messages

Could not connect to device
Make sure your OBD-II adapter is plugged in and the ignition is turned to ON. Then try again.

Success Messages

✓ Scan complete!

Instructional Text

💡 Why enter a VIN?
The VIN helps us decode exact trim, engine, and specs. You can skip this and enter details manually.

Capitalization & Formatting

  • Use sentence case for most text: "Safe to buy"
  • Use title case for navigation and proper features: "Vehicle Scan Report"
  • Use ALL CAPS sparingly, only for VIN codes: "VIN: 1HGBH41JXMN109186"
  • Button text uses sentence case: "Continue to scan"
  • Numbers under 10 can be spelled out in prose, but use numerals in data: "3 trouble codes"

Technical Terms

When technical terms are necessary, provide context:

  • Good: "OBD-II (On-Board Diagnostics) scanner"
  • Good: "P0171 — Fuel system too lean"
  • Good: "Catalytic converter efficiency below threshold"
